Tips on How to Sound More Intelligent

Below are a couple of tips to help you sound like you know something.

  • Tip 1
  • Tip 2
  • Tip 3
  • Tip 4
Make your sentences as long as possible. The longer and more complicated they are, the better.
Use less slang and more complicated words. Don't use filler words such as 'like'.
Put the verb at the beginning of your sentence. Often it makes you sound more intelligent if you put the detail and subject before the verb when you communicate.
Don't use contractions such as 'don't'. Instead say the full version. In the case of 'don't' it would be 'do not'.

Conversation Starters

Here are some great coversation starters to help you get the talk rolling like a pro. These were taken from the Huffington Post and translated into Pride and Prejudice fancy language as best as possible.

  • Happiness
  • Embarassing Moment
  • Job
Gazing back onto your whole life, the moment you possesed the most happiness would be where along that line? What was the previously mentioned moment?
What moment, taking into account your entire life, can you remember giving you the most social distress whilst conversing, helping, or in some other way interacting with someone else?
At this current moment in time what is your current occupation, and how does it compare and/or relate to other occupations that you have had in the past? Is this a line of work that you wish to pursue in the future, or do you plan on relinquishing this job to somebody that's not yourself?
